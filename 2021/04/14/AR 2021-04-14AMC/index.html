SOME RECORDS ON AMC and AIME

Posted on 2021-04-14

On 5th and 11th February, I finished my two AMC exams.

AMC is American Mathematics Competition. It is one of the most popular and welcomed maths competition all over the world. During primary school, I used to take part in AMC8 and receive a relative high score(Top 2.5%). As a result, after attending senior high school, AMC became one of my after-class activities as well, because I wanted to challenge myself through it.

During my process of preparation, I felt a little bit lonely from time to time, as there were only a few students in my class choosing to try AMC12 which is beyond the current syllabus.

I tried to talk with August who prepared for AMC12 too, about the exam preparing. We discussed questions from time to time after that. Now, we are good friends. This is the most impressive part of the activity.

I started to understand that although we may compete with each other during the competition, it is still important and possible for us to cooperate, as a result of friendship. Competition is more than competing. August and I received satisfied scores. I got 103.5points in AMC10A and 117points in AMC12B.

Additionally, through AMC, one thing has become clearer than before. The process of improving, challenging and studying may be lonely because everyone makes different decision during this period. For example, my best friend Estella chose to forgo AMC as she wanted to take part in FBLA more. Another example is that during the last preparing day, August and I still had a lot of things which we had not learnt and understood. We chose to study different areas.

From my point of view, the aloneness is one of the lessons we need to learn during our studying time.

Fortunately, I got the chance to take part in AIME(America Invitational Mathematics Examination). However, something unexpected happened. The date of AIME was conflicted with my school IGCSE mock examination. I said to myself several time, “Mock is more important as it is the first time for me to experience international examinations hold by CAIE and mock exam will be the source of GPA of this semester.” Every time the sense of not convinced also came to my heart as it really took me hours and hours learning maths knowledge and going through past papers.

Finally, I decided to give up preparing for AIME to save time for mock exams. After handing in my AIME paper, I got to know that this time I must have been failed to achieve anything. It was near 8 p.m. when AIME was done. Block 1912 opposite my school was still full of music sounds and colourful lights. Suddenly, one concept which I learnt in Economics came to my mind——opportunity cost and made me feel relived.

Everyone has limited time and energy, but I always long for finishing everything which is unattainable and impossible. As a result, it is necessary for me to make a decision on what to do. Opportunity cost is the next best choice foregone when making a decision. I still remember the words which were said by my Economics teacher Ms. Cindy when I told her that I would switch my future major from Physics to Economics. She said that, “Every decision or choice has an opportunity cost. I simply hope that you can consider the thing carefully before making a decision.”

Perhaps to Estella, she though FBLA was more important than AMC so she made such a decision that high prize was rewarded to her by FBLA but not so good performance was shown by her in AMC. To me, I still hope to achieve higher marks in IGCSE instead of AIME.

To sum up, I will always remember AMC and AIME of this year. They provides me with a chance to experience the concept of opportunity costs by myself instead of leaving it only as a piece of knowledge in my Economics books.

Meantime, after seeing the lights and hearing the sounds from Block 1912, I am not regretted about my decision anymore, although a total of only 4 marks was achieved by me in AIME, because I still realised that life sucks, so it may be better for me to be cute and enjoy and try to get understand the tastes of life more, instead of giving myself too much press to spend time seeing the around world.
