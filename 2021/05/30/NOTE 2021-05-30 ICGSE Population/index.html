Section4 Mainly on Population

Posted on 2021-05-30

Factors affected

Natural increase

  • Birth rate

    • Definition: Birth rate is the number of births in a year per 1000 population in a year

    • Result

      • The higher a country’s birth rate, the greater its population growth will tend to be.

    • Causes of high birth rate

      • Women are not well-educated and they don’t work
      • It is cheap to bring up children
      • Lack or disapproval of family planning
      • Government cash incentives to have children but a lack of government help to care for the sick and elderly

    • Similar measure

      • Fertility rate
      • Infant mortality rate

  • Death rate

    • Definition: It is the number of the deaths in a year per 1000 population a year

      • Better-quality education, healthcare, nutrition and sanitation, people in MEDCs tend to live longer.
      • Famine, poverty, poor housing, high infant mortality rates and diseases tend to reduce life expectancy in LEDCs.

    • Result

      • The lower the death rate, the more population the country has

    • Causes of low death rate

      • Healthy diets
      • Good housing facilities
      • Access to high quality medical care
      • Healthy lifestyles
      • Enjoy good working conditions
      • Non-involvement in military action

Net migration

  • Components

    • Definition: It is the difference between immigration and emigration
    • Definition: Immigration occurs when people enter a country to live and work
    • Definition: Emigration occurs when people leave a country to live and work abroad.

  • Results

    • Net immigration

      • There is an excess in immigration

    • Net emigration

      • There is an excess in emigration

      • Consequences

        • Disadvantages

          • Size of working population is likely to be reduced
          • A greater burden of dependency
          • Average age of the labour force may increase and make it less mobile
          • Gender distribution may be influenced
          • A shortage of skilled workers
          • Under-utilisation of resources. The country may become under-populated

        • Advantages

          • Emigrations may send money home to help their relatives to increase the living standards

  • Causes of net migration

    • Relative living standards at home and abroad
    • Persecution of particular groups
    • Extent of control on the movement of people

Changes

In size

  • Optimum age

    • Definition: It is the size of the population which maximises the country’s output per head

    • The balance between population and resources is of crucial importance

    • Results

      • Under population

        • If it does not have enough of human resources to make the best use of its resources

      • Over population

        • If there is a shortage of land, capital and technical knowledge, relative to the number of workers

    • It is hard to determine optimum age because the quantity and quality of resources are changing all the time

  • Consequences of population growth

    • Advantages

      • If the population is below the optimum size, the country can make better use of its resource

      • Market size will increase. It is easier for firms to take advantages of economies of scale

      • Increase factor mobility. If the growth is caused by an increase in birth rate or immigration, these people are familiar with new ideas and methods. Also, costs of training reduce

      • Demand will increase so investment may be stimulated and new technology may be introduced

      • A rise in labour force

        • Net migration will bring in more workers
        • More children will increase labour force in the long run, but in short run it will increase dependency ratio

    • Disadvantages

      • Concerns about famine. If the country is suffering from overpopulated and low agricultural productivity, the risk of more dependents appears

      • Restrictions on improvements in living standards. Resources which could have been used to improve living standards may be devoted to the provision of necessities(OC)

      • Overcrowding. Put pressure on housing and social capital and cause traffic congestion

      • Environmental pressure. May damage to wildlife habitats, water shortages and the depletion of non-renewable resources

      • Pressure on employment opportunities.

        • If there is an increase in the number of people of working age who lack skills(immigration), the government may need to pay more for education and training
        • *Remember: Immigration does not cause employment.(The number of job is not fixed) Immigration will increase the supply of labour and AD, if there is no available job or they have better skills, unemployment may appear.

      • Balance of payment pressures. More dependants may results in a rise in imports and some products may need to be diverted from the export to the home market

  • Ways to reducing birth rate

    • Improve the educational and employment opportunities for women
    • Improve healthcare and nutrition to reduce infant mortality and birth rate
    • Set up pension and sickness insurance schemes to reduce the need for family support
    • Raising school leaving age to raise costs of having children
    • Reduce or stop any financial support it gives to families for each child and provide incentives for families who restrict the number of children
    • More extreme way is to make it illegal to have children above a specified number

In structure

  • Gender

    • Gender distribution indicates the number of males compared to the number of females
    • In most countries, more boys are born than girls

  • Age

    • Age distribution is the division of the population into different age groups

    • Image

      • Population pyramids

        • Population pyramids are a graphical representation of the age and gender distribution of a country’s population.

    • Results

      • Low-income countries tend to have a relatively larger proportion of their population in the younger age groups.
      • Wealthier countries tend to have an ageing population with a growing number of people in older age groups.

    • Ageing population

      • Causes

        • A fall in birth rate
        • A fall in death rate
        • Net emigration

      • Consequences

        • A rise in dependency ratio
        • A change in the labour force. Older workers may be geographically and occupationally less mobile. More experienced, reliable and patient.
        • Higher demand for healthcare
        • Greater need for welfare services
        • Rise in cost of state and private pensions
        • Change in the pattern of demand

      • Ways to coping

        • Raise tax burden on workers
        • Raise the retirement age to reduce costs of pension and increase tax revenue
        • Encourage or make it compulsory for workers to save for their retirement
        • Raise productivity of workers by means of education and training to raise taxes
        • Encourage the immigration of younger skilled workers by issuing more work permits

  • Area

    • Internal migration

      • Workers migrate from rural to urban to search for better jobs or higher incomes.

      • Consequences

        • Advantages

          • Migrants may supply growing industries and result in a better allocation of resources
          • Raise living standards in rural area due to a decrease in initial unemployment and money sent back

        • Disadvantages

          • If the most productive workers leave, agricultural productivity may fall
          • May not available jobs in urban areas
          • External costs including congesting

Dependency ratio

  • It is the proportion of the population that has to be supported by the labour force

  • Formula: number in dependent age groups/number in the labour force*100

  • Causes of increase

    • Higher birth rates which mainly occurs in less economically developed countries
    • A high school leaving age
    • Social changes such as workers entering the labour force at a later stage due to greater demand for higher education
    • More people choose early retirement
