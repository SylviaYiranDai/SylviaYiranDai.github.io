Section1 The Basic Economic Problems

Posted on 2021-05-30

The nature of the economic problem

Needs vs Wants

  • Needs are essential goods and services required for survival
  • Wants are goods and services that are not necessary for survival

Economics goods vs Free good

  • Economics goods have opportunity cost.
  • Definition: Economic goods is a product which requires resources to produce it and therefore has an opportunity cost. EG. carpet
  • Free goods don’t have opportunity cost.
  • Definition: Free goods is a product which dos not require any resources to make it and so does not have an opportunity cost. EG. sunshine, water in a river
    • If the water is processed for drinking or irrigation of fields, it becomes an economic goods.

Economic problem

  • Definition: The people’s wants are unlimited, but the resources are limited.( Scarcity )
  • Everyone has to make choices because of scarcity.

Opportunity cost

Resource allocation therefore involves deciding how best to use scarce resources to satisfy as many needs and wants as possible.

Definition: Opportunity cost is the cost of choice. It means the best alternative foregone when making a choice. It is the cost measured in terms of the next best choice given up when making a choice.

Production possibility curve

Definition: PPC shows the maximum combined output of two or more products a firm or an entire economy can produce with its available resources.

locations of points

  • on the curve

    • efficient
    • attainable

  • inside the curve

    • inefficient
    • attainable

  • outside the curve

    • unattainable

shift

  • outside

    • increase in resources
    • increase in productivity
    • improve technology

  • inside

    • decrease in the productive capacity( quantity or quality) of a country

PPCs are therefore a useful way of showing the opportunity cost of producing more of one product in terms of how much of another must be given up

Factors of production

LAND

[SYN] Natural Resources

  • Definition: Land is gifts of nature available for production. eg. land itself, coal, rainforests, fish
  • Renewable & Unrenewable
  • Rewards: Rent

LABOUR

[SYN] Human capital

  • Definition: Labour is the human effort used in producing goods and services.
  • Improved productivity—-Technology, education and training
  • Rewards: Wages

CAPITAL

  • Definition: Capital is human-made goods used in production. eg. offices, factories, machinery, railway and tools.

  • Capital goods vs Consumer goods

    • determine who is the user and the purpose of its use

  • Rewards: Interest

ENTERPRISE

  • Definition: Enterprise is the skill a business person required to combine and manage successfully the other three factors of production and ability to undertake risk and to make decisions in business. （Do not include managers)
  • Rewards: Profits

Mobility

  • Land

    • occupationally mobile
    • geographically immobile

  • Labour

    • occupationally immobile

      • lack of information
      • lack of appropriate skills and qualifications

    • geographically immobile

      • differences in the price and availability of housing in different areas and countries
      • family ties
      • differences in educational systems in different areas and countries
      • lack of information
      • restrictions on the movement of workers

  • Capital

    • varies according to the type of capital goods

  • Enterprise(Entrepreneurs)

    • Most mobile FOP
