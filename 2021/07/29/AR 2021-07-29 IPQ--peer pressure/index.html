Some records on IPQ -- Thinking on peer pressure

Posted on 2021-07-29

During the summer holiday, I attended some class to complete my Independent Project Qualification (IPQ). There are several students from other cities like Shanghai, Beijing or Shenzhen. We took lessons together.

One day, I had dinner with some students from Ulink College which is a famous A-Level school in Shanghai. They were talking about their study of A-Level. I was so surprised that, we are students of the same grades. However, they have already finished what I will study in the future two years. I was deeply influenced and felt anxious, stressed and worries. After that, I spent several hours thinking about what I can do in the future to make myself being able to compete with them.

My teacher Ms. Diao told me this was the result of peer pressure.

What is peer pressure? Wikipedia defines it as the direct influence on people by peers, or the effect on an individual who is encouraged and wants to follow their peers by changing their attitudes, values or behaviours to conform to those of the influencing group or individual.

To be honest, I have been suffered from peer pressure even since I was in my junior high school. At that time, I was always the second place in my class. It seemed that it was impossible for me to become the first. As a result, during the final year, the question which was whether I could become the first in class appeared in my mind everyday. After becoming a student in senior high school, I always worried about getting lower scores than my classmates or attending fewer activities than my classmates, because these will make me weaker than them when applying for universities.

Why I have peer pressure? This is the question coming to my mind, because I know when solving some problems, it is important for us to know the cause of the problems.

We are living in the society and are members of many organisations. For example, I am a member of my class. At the same time, I am a member of my family, my school, my project teams. Partners act important roles in my life. My friends in class are my partners. My parents are my partners as well. But I know that my friends and my parents are of huge difference. They are in different ages. They have different relationship with me. They provide me with and I give them different things.

Peers are our partners who are in similar ages with us.

I never worry about why I can not do the same things as my parents at present, but I used to be trapped in the problem why I could not achieve same grades in school as my parents did when they were in school. This is another kind of peer pressure.

As I mentioned above, we are members of many organisations. This provides us with a platform to see people who have connection with us. It is almost human-beings’ instinct to compare, because everyone wants to prove that themselves are the best. There is an old saying in China—“The beauty is loved by all”. We hope that we are the beauty because we are a member of “all” as well.

Peer pressure is everywhere in our daily life. We could not help us comparing with others in all aspects.

When looking at the consequences of peer pressure, things become more interesting. Some people say that peer pressure provides them with energy to continue working, while others think that it stops them from becoming better and better because of the heavy heart of gain and loss.

Actually, everyone pays attention to different things. Nichole, my best friend, worries about her emotions more, because she has been suffered from bipolar affective disorder for a long time. Yuki, my dest mate, may worry about the relationship between her boyfriend and her more. By comparison, I pay most of my attention to my study, including the grades, the activities, the achievements, the competitions and so on. This is the reason why after listening talks to the students from Ulink I became anxious and stressed.

I know that we are in different platform because we are students from different schools. I know that they are in Shanghai which is a more developed city than Nanjing. I know that they have been in the international system of education longer than me. However, I do hope that I can do as well as them instead of falling behind them.

In order to solve this problem, I talked to my junior high school Physics teacher Ms. Jiao, my senior high school Economics and Business teacher Ms. Cindy, and my counsellor Ms. Diao. They all hope me to pay less attention to others and just be myself, instead of being influenced by others. From my point of view, this is almost impossible for me. I made up my mind to develop several hobbies and separate my attention. Also, I will try my best to become better and better.

Ms. Diao’s parting advice to me during my birthday is very impressive, “Take it easy. Grow up slowly.”
