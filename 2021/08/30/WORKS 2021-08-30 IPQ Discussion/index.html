This situation appears because United Airlines still need to pay high fixed costs, including salaries and related costs, depreciation and amortisation, lading fees and other rent, and regional capacity purchase. It may take several years to cover the loss because this situation may continue in 2021, and the recovery is relatively slow. It is hard for it to gain it again in the recession environment in the whole society. In conclusion, in 2020, the profitability of United Airlines is negative.

II. Provide suggestions on future development

The following part will use SPACE Matrix Analysis which stands for Strategic Position and ACtion Evaluation Matrix. We use SPACE Matrix Analysis to work out the strategy of United Airlines in the future. It contains four dimensions, including financial strength, competitive advantage, industry strength and environmental stability. The former two are the internal ones, while the latter two are the external ones. Each dimension has around seven indicators. In the following part, we will discuss each dimension. At the beginning of each dimension, we will show all the dimensions’ indicators and then discuss them respectively. All the indicators will be given a point from 0 to 6 to evaluate the level of each indicator. After discussing all the indicators of one dimension, we will calculate the average point of all the indicators. Four dimensions together will show the result.

We will firstly talk about Environmental stability. It has many indicators, including technological changes, inflation rate, demand variability, the price range of competing products, barriers to entry into the market, competitive pressure, and the price elasticity of demand (Radder, 1998).

Technological change is a shift to a new production function resulting from an increase in the efficiency of a product or process. The shift is the primary source of potential economic growth, which an outward shift of the Productive Possibility Curve of a company, an industry, or the whole economy can represent. United Airlines highly depends on technology. It is reported that in 2021 the Company booked 25 Boeing 737 MAX aircraft (United Airlines, 2021). Also, many media said that the Company had an upcoming IT investment (Malone, 2021). I will give point 1 to technological changes.

Competitive pressure affects a company’s incentives to undertake product and process innovations (Boone, 2000). The aviation industry is a highly competitive market, although we mentioned above that it is an oligopoly. So I will give point 1 to competitive pressure.

The price range of competing products is another indicator. Competing products can be understood as substitutes. There are lots of substitutes for the products of United Airlines. For example, the airline services provided by Delta Air Lines and American Airlines. There are still many other ways of transportation like coaches and trains. So I will give point 1 to the price range of competing.

Demand variability is a measure of how much variability there is in customer demand. We assume that the demand variability is relatively large because of the availability of substitutes and the low degree of necessities. I will give point 2 to demand variability.

Price elasticity of demand (PED) measures the responsiveness of the quantity demanded for a product when the price changes. The formula is: . We can not obtain accurate data about PED, but we can estimate it. We assume that during COVID-19, the demand for airline services did not change while their supply decreased due to travel restrictions. Quantity demanded for them decreased as well. At the same time, the revenue dropped. So the demand for them may be price elastic. There are several other indicators. For example, close substitutes are available, including the train and ship. However, it is likely to take up a high proportion of people’s incomes. So I will give point 2 to price elasticity of demand.

The rate of inflation, which is an increase in general price level and a decrease in purchasing power, has many indicators, including Consumer Price Index and Wholesale Price Index. The inflation rate was 1.81% in 2019, 1.25% in 2020, and was predicted to be 2.26% in 2021 (Statista, 2021). These rates are not high inflation rates, as FED set the inflation target at 2% (FED, 2021). I will give point 3 to the rate of inflation.

When discussing the aviation industry, the barriers to entry into the market are relatively high because this industry is capital-intensive. It requires the investors to buy huge capital goods, including aircraft, which need a considerable amount of money. It is defined as an oligopoly by FED because mergers between 2005 and 2015 decreased the number of airline companies significantly. After that, four airlines controlled 80 per cent of the US market in 2015 (Mutzabaugh, 2015). Additionally, the competition for airport gates and significant economies of scale increase the barriers (FED, 2018). So I will give point 5 to barriers to entry.

In conclusion, the average point for environmental stability is approximately 2.14.

Then, we will discuss industry strength. It includes many indicators like growth potential, profit potential, financial stability, technological know-how, resource utilisation, capital intensity, ease of entry into the market, productivity or capacity utilisation, and flexibility or adaptability (Radder, 1998).

Capital intensity is one of the indicators of barriers to entry mentioned above. The aviation industry is capital intensive because of the high demand for aircraft to transport people and products. As this industry is capital intensive, I will give it point 1 for capital intensity.

A stable financial system can provide vital services to households and businesses in good times and bad (Bank of England, 2019). Because of COVID-19, it is hard for United Airlines to control its revenue, expenditure and cash flow. Also, the cash flow performed poorly in 2020. So I will give it point 1.

Productivity utilisation is the extent to which a firm or nation employee its installed productive capacity. As we can find from the annual report (United Airlines, 2021), the number of passengers and available seat miles, the number of seats available for passengers multiplied by the number of scheduled miles those seats flown, can be used.

The profit potential is the potential for a product to generate revenue which, after expenses, leads to net income. As we are considering the profitability of United Airlines in the near future, the travel restrictions may not be loosened in the short run due to the continuous COVID-19 pandemic. It may keep negative profitability in 2021, similar to 2020, which is mentioned above. So I will give it point 2.

Technological know-how is the set of knowledge and skills that participants have developed and used to guide the acquisition, creation and operation of computer-based systems that enable or facilitate the performance of business processes (Hartono, 2011). As the aviation industry has exactly simple technology, I will give it point 2.

The flexibility of United Airlines is hard to get. However, the civil aviation industry is a member of the tertiary sector, which means it mainly provides customers with services instead of goods. Services can be customised. Meanwhile, the unique point of the aviation industry is that although it is in the tertiary sector, it is still a capital-intensive industry. Sometimes it is hard for them to respond to customers’ wants. For example, the company can not create a new airline in the short term. So I will give point 3 for it.

Growth potential is the future ability of an organisation to gain higher profits, expand the workforce and increase output (Lebeaux, 2012). United Airlines booked up to 25 Boeing 737 MAX aircraft in 2021 (United Airlines, 2021). It is highly possible to grow in the future because of the higher productive potential caused by more capital goods. I will give it point 4.

Resource utilisation means the extent of the use of the resources, which is hard to measure. However, we estimate that as United Airlines is capital intensive, it is highly possible to use its capital resources thoroughly. It is relatively efficient. So I will give it point 5.

It is not easy for investors to enter into aviation market. As mentioned above, it has high start-up costs to purchase capital goods, including aircraft, which increase the barriers to enter the market significantly. So I will give it point 5 for the ease of entry into the market.

In conclusion, the overall mark for industry strength is approximately 2.667.

Then I will talk about Competitive advantage. It includes indicators like market share, product quality, product life cycle, product replacement cycle, customer loyalty, competition’s capacity, technological know-how and vertical integration (Radder, 1998).

As United Airlines is in the tertiary sector, vertical integration means it merges with the providers of the raw materials. However, it seems that United Airlines is fond of horizontal integration. So I will give it point 2.

Technological know-how is mentioned above. The point is 2.

In 2020, Southwest had the largest market share at 19.78% in the domestic market, while Delta Air Lines had the second-largest market share at 14.37%. America Airlines and United Airlines followed the previous two at 16.83% and 8.92%, respectively. It is the fourth-largest airline company in the US. I will give it point 4 in market share.

It seems that product quality is hard to measure because it is the personal feeling about the services provided by United Airlines. However, there is still statistic on the consumers’ feelings of the product quality of United Airlines. It is approximately 3.3 out of 5 points (Comparably, 2021), while American Airlines has 3.6 and British Airways has 3.6. I will give point 4 on the product quality.

Competition’s capacity is more about the degree of competition in the industry. As the number of airline companies is relatively low in both the USA and the world market, we consider the aviation industry as an oligopoly market. I will give point 4 because the level of competition is relatively high.

The product life cycle is relatively long. Also, it has not been changed for years and is predicted to remain in the future because of a lack of substitutes and a high degree of necessity. So the point is 4.

It is noticeable that United Airlines has a unique program called MileagePlus designed to accumulate the miles consumers travel to increase customer loyalty (United Airlines, 2021). This program produced $1.8 billion in earnings last year before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortisation. It also said that the program had more than 100 million members. I will give point 6 to customer loyalty.

The overall grade for competitive advantage is 3.375.

Finally, we will look at the financial strength. It contains many indicators, including the return on investment, leverage, liquidity, capital required, cash flow, ease of exit from the market and risk involved in the business.

Capital available is another important indicator. As United Airlines operates in the aviation industry, it is capital intensive. So the point for it is 1.

When looking at ease of exit from the market, the critical point is the barriers to exit. It may be hard for United Airlines to leave the market because it owns many capital goods, including aircraft. Also, it has much debt owed to investors, so United Airlines may not be able to find a buyer to buy all the aircraft and repay the debts. Meanwhile, the depreciation of the aircraft may make it challenging to repay the debts even they find a buyer. I will give point 1 on ease of exit from the market.

As we mentioned above, when looking at the cash flow statement of United Airlines, we found that it performed very bad in 2020. The free cash flow was negative. This trend may continue in the future years because of COVID-19 and low net income. I will give it point 1.

Although based on the theory, it should leave the industry.
